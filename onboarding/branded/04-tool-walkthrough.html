01 · What you'll need

Before you start your first match.

Set up once. Reuse for every match after.

  • The match video — mp4 or shared link from Joshua.
  • The match metadata — jersey colours, opponent, date, video_job_id. Usually one message.
  • A video player that supports frame-by-frame. VLC works: E = next frame, D = previous frame. Browser players usually do not.
  • Excel (or Google Sheets / LibreOffice — Excel preserves the dropdowns best).
  • A local clone of the repo, or read access to scripts/ground-truth/_template.xlsx.
  • Node.js if you'll run the converter yourself; otherwise hand the .xlsx to Joshua.

Time budget

A typical match takes 60–90 minutes of labeling for ~50 minutes of video. The first match you label will take longer — expect 2-3 hours. By match 10 you'll be at the typical rate.

If you're billing per match, the rate is calibrated to ~1.5× experienced-labeler time. So a senior labeler earns a premium on speed. New labelers are expected to be slower — that's normal.

Recommended setup
Two monitors or wide screen Video on one side, Excel on the other. Single-monitor labelers tend to mis-timestamp. Hotkey for pause Set it at the home row. You'll use it constantly. VLC frame-step E = next frame, D = previous frame. VLC playback speed + = faster, – = slower. 1.5× is comfortable for review passes; 0.5× for save classification.

02 · The end-to-end workflow

Same five steps, every match.

01

Set up the workbook

Copy scripts/ground-truth/_template.xlsx to scripts/ground-truth/<match-name>.xlsx. Naming: <keeper>-vs-<opponent>-YYYY-MM-DD.xlsx. Open in Excel. Fill Metadata sheet first. Confirm colours match the analyzer config (ask Joshua if unclear).

02

Blind pass on the video

Open the video. Note duration on Metadata. Play. When something happens, pause and log. Use frame-by-frame (VLC: E / D) to confirm exact strike timestamp, shooter's jersey colour at contact, keeper's hand shape if visible. After halftime, explicitly check you're still labeling.

03

Compare against the Gemini output

Once blind pass is complete, get the Gemini JSON from Joshua / the queue. Side-by-side. Events Gemini found that you missed: verify on tape, ADD if real, skip if phantom. Events you found that Gemini missed: keep — these are the training-leverage cases. Disagreements on classification: rubric wins. Do NOT just copy Gemini's labels.

04

Self-check

Run the checklist from 03a Rubric §09. Don't skip. Two minutes here prevents an hour of back-and-forth.

05

Convert and submit

Run node scripts/excel-to-ground-truth.js scripts/ground-truth/<match-name>.xlsx. Eyeball the JSON. Goal count matches? Saves array looks right? Commit both files, or hand off to Joshua if you don't have repo write access.

03 · Known Gemini failure modes

Five documented mistakes to spot.

From the changelog in prompts/README.md. Knowing these helps you recognize phantoms when comparing against the Gemini output.

Table 03.1 · Gemini failure modes & how to spot them
#Failure modeYour defence
01 Inventing saves on dominant-win matches. 50+ saves reported for a match with 4 actual shots. Apply the antecedent-attack test. If preceding_attack is vague, the save is invented.
02 Flipping the scoring team. 100% wrong historically when reporting "analyzed team conceded"; 50% wrong on opposition. Always verify by the shooter's kit colour at the moment of strike. Compare to evidence_shooter_color field.
03 Drifting timestamps. Reported events at the wrong time, or events that don't exist at all. Always use your own video-offset timestamp from frame-stepping. Never copy Gemini's timestamps.
04 Logging opposition keeper distributions. When our keeper is off-camera, sometimes attributes opposition releases to us. Colour-check every distribution. The keeper kit colour at the moment of release is the test.
05 Conflating distance with kick type. "GK Long Kick" labels on goal kicks; "Throw" labels on 40-yard balls. Use the release-motion decision tree (see 03a §05b). Distance is the LAST step, not the first.

We are not measuring "how well a human agrees with Gemini." We are measuring ground truth. The model is wrong; your job is to be right.

04 · Loom video shot lists

Two videos, ten minutes each.

When recorded, these will live in this section. Until then, the shot lists below are the script.

Video 1 — "Filling in a ground truth doc from scratch"

Target length: 10-12 minutes

  • 0:00 Open the template, save-as with the match slug.
  • 1:00 Fill Metadata while talking through where each value comes from.
  • 2:30 Open the video, note duration on Metadata.
  • 3:00 Walk through 3-4 events in real time: one goal (with frame-stepping for shooter colour), one save (showing antecedent-attack test), one distribution (release-motion decision), one "I cannot tell" moment.
  • 10:00 Run the converter, eyeball the JSON, commit.
Recording notes
Don't script rigidly. Talking through real decisions on real tape is more useful than a polished script.

Use a recent labeled match where you remember the gray areas. Show the labeler the moment of doubt and explain how you ruled.

Keep the camera off your face — screen recording only. Voice over.

Video 2 — "Reviewing the Gemini output as a second check"

Target length: 10 minutes

  • 0:00 Show the Gemini JSON side-by-side with your labeled JSON.
  • 1:00 Walk through three disagreements: one where Gemini was right and you missed it; one where Gemini was wrong (a phantom save) — verify and skip; one classified differently — explain rubric reasoning.
  • 6:00 Show the 5 known failure modes from the previous page with real examples.
  • 9:00 Submit + final checklist.

05 · Shortcuts & when stuck

Tools and shortcuts that pay back fast.

Table 05.1 · The shortcuts that matter
Tool / shortcutWhy it pays
VLC frame-step · E next, D previousLock the strike timestamp to the exact frame. Use this constantly.
VLC playback speed · + faster, - slower1.5× for review passes; 0.5× for save classification.
Excel "Fill down"When 6 backpasses in a row are all Pass / Short to defender / Defender, drag the fill handle. Saves minutes per match.
Dual monitorVideo on one side, Excel on the other. Single-monitor labelers tend to mis-timestamp.
Pause hotkey at home rowDon't reach for spacebar. Use a hotkey close to your typing position.

When you're stuck

If you've watched a moment three times and still can't classify it, mark it Unclear and move on. Don't burn 5 minutes on one event. The reviewer would rather see 10 Unclear rows than 5 confidently-wrong rows.

If you're stuck on something philosophical ("does this count as a save?"), add it to the Edge-Case Log (03b) with what you saw, what you ruled (best guess), and flag it for review. It gets resolved within the day and becomes canon for future labelers.

Three habits good labelers share
Default to Unclear Honest beats confident. Describe, don't infer Observable beats imagined. Add edge cases to the log Your gray area is the next labeler's clear rule.
