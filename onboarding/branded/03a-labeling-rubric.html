01 · Workflow

For each match, in order.

The labeling tool is one Excel workbook per match generated from the template at scripts/ground-truth/_template.xlsx. Seven sheets, dropdown validation, sample row in each.

01

Get the video and metadata

Joshua provides the video link, jersey colours, opponent name, date, and video_job_id.

02

Generate a fresh template

Copy _template.xlsx to scripts/ground-truth/<match-name>.xlsx. Naming: <keeper>-vs-<opponent>-YYYY-MM-DD.xlsx.

03

Fill Metadata first

Get the colours right. Labels downstream depend on this. Match colours must match what was sent to the analyzer.

04

Watch blind, top to bottom

Do NOT look at the Gemini output before labeling. Anchoring on the model's first pass is the #1 mistake. We're measuring ground truth — not how well a human agrees with Gemini.

05

Review the Gemini output as a second check

Events Gemini found that you missed: verify on tape, add if real. Events you found that Gemini missed: keep. Events you both found differently: rubric wins.

06

Run the self-check (page 14)

Two minutes here prevents an hour of back-and-forth. Don't skip.

07

Convert and submit

Run node scripts/excel-to-ground-truth.js scripts/ground-truth/<match-name>.xlsx. Eyeball the JSON. Commit or hand off.

Universal timestamp rules

  • All timestamps are video offsets in MM:SS from the start of the file. Not match clock.
  • Mark the moment the ball is struck, not the moment the keeper touches it.
  • If unsure within ±5 seconds, drop confidence to low.
  • Two events within 5 seconds of each other are suspicious — either double-counted, or a rebound (asymmetry: two save events, one goal).

Labeling Rubric

Need 2 of: kickoff within 60s · celebration within 10s · scoreboard +1. Less = don't log.
Rebound rule
Rebounds collapse to one goal at the rebound shot's timestamp.

03 · Goals sheet

One row per goal.

Rebounds collapse to one goal at the rebound shot's timestamp. Replays don't count. Identify the shooter by jersey colour at the moment of strike.

Hard rules

  • Two-of-three evidence. Every goal needs at least 2: kickoff within 60s after, celebration within 10s, or scoreboard +1. Fewer = don't log.
  • Shooter's jersey decides scoring team. Observed at the moment of strike. Not celebration colour.
  • Rebounds collapse. One goal at the rebound shot's timestamp.
  • Replays don't count. No kickoff + no scoreboard change → it's a replay of one already logged.

Every save row needs a real opposition attack in 3-5s before the shot. No describable attack → drop the row.
Off-target
Log them. On target: No, GK action: Unclear, Outcome: Out of play.

One row per release.

High volume. Be thorough. Classify by release motion, not by distance.

Traps

  • Goal kicks are Pass. Ball on ground at strike. NOT GK Long.
  • A "long throw" doesn't exist. Throws don't go >25 yards.
  • Backpasses returned by foot are Pass. Ball on ground → pass.
  • Opposition keeper distributions are NOT logged. Colour-check every release.

05b · Distribution-type decision tree

Apply in order. Stop at the first match.

01

Ball leaves the keeper's hands?

→ Throw

02

Ball ON THE GROUND when foot struck?

→ Pass

03

Keeper DROPS ball, strikes BEFORE ground?

→ GK Short Kick (<25 yards) or GK Long Kick (>25 yards). Motion identical; distance decides.

04

Keeper drops, lets it BOUNCE, strikes rising ball?

→ Drop-kick

When to log here vs. the Saves sheet

A 1v1 that produced a shot still gets logged on the Saves sheet as the shot event. The 1v1 sheet captures the moment as a whole — keeper's positioning, angle, decision, recovery. Both sheets can have a row for the same physical event from different angles. That's intentional — the dashboard cross-references them.

A through ball where the keeper sprinted out, cleared at the edge of the box, no shot taken — that's a Sweeper event, not a 1v1. The 1v1 sheet is for moments where an attacker had a shot opportunity.

09 · Self-check before you submit

Run through this list before converting to JSON.

Three groups of checks. Tick mentally as you go through each.

Goals
  • Every goal has kickoff + celebration + scoreboard confirmation (at least 2 of 3)
  • Scoring team matches the shooter's observed jersey colour (not celebration colour)
  • No two goals within 5 seconds of each other (rebounds collapse to one)
  • Final score equals sum of Us goals vs sum of Opponent goals
Saves
  • Every save row has a Play description that describes a real opposition attack
  • Rebound chains are logged as separate rows (Block + Goal, not one row)
  • Off-target shots have On target: No and GK action: Unclear
  • No Catch row where the ball wasn't retained — those are Block or Parry
  • No Parry row where the redirect direction isn't visible — those are Deflect or Unclear
  • Goal placement side uses GK perspective (GK Left / Centre / GK Right)
Distribution
  • No goal kicks labeled as GK Long Kick (goal kicks are Pass)
  • No throws over ~25 yards (those are GK Long or Drop-kick)
  • No opposition-keeper distributions logged
  • Multi-touch possessions logged as ONE event at the release timestamp

09b · Coverage & honesty

Two final groups.

Coverage
  • Events distributed across the full match duration, not just the first 15 minutes (unless the match was so one-sided one team had no second-half activity)
  • You actually watched the second half (this sounds obvious — set yourself a halftime check)
Honesty
  • You used Unclear freely where appropriate. If your match has zero Unclear entries, you may have over-classified — re-check borderline calls.
  • You added any edge cases you ruled on to 03b-edge-case-log.md

Once all checks pass

Run the converter:

node scripts/excel-to-ground-truth.js scripts/ground-truth/<match-name>.xlsx

Eyeball the JSON. Goal count matches? Saves array looks right? Then commit (or hand off to Joshua if you don't have repo write access).

An honest 'I couldn't tell' beats a confident guess every time.
